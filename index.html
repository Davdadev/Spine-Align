Product Materials

Core Electronic Components:
Arduino Pro Mini (5V recommended for simplicity), MPU6050 accelerometer/gyroscope module,
Sound system / buzzer (small piezo or speaker, rated for Arduino voltage), Mini slide switch (ON/OFF),
Power Bank, to keep the device running when on back (lightweight),
Buck/boost converter (only if needed for voltage adjustment — usually not required)

Wiring & Assembly Parts:
Jumper wires or thin flexible silicone-coated small plastic enclosure or 3D-printed casing,
Velcro straps or elastic bands (for attaching to body/clothing), Sewing kit or fabric glue (if embedding in clothing),
Hot glue gun (to secure components in casing)

Programming & Setup:
USB-to-Serial adapter (FTDI) for programming Arduino Pro Mini,
Computer with Arduino IDE installed, MPU6050 Arduino library (Jeff Rowberg’s version recommended)

Evaluation

When we set out to make our product, we quickly ran into challenges with programming. The coding and technology behind the device were more advanced than we had expected, especially since we didn’t have much experience. While it works, the device is more fragile than we had hoped. It can get damaged easily and needs a strong casing, like cardboard or a 3D-printed cover, for protection. The MPU6050 sensor also isn’t as robust as we would like. Looking back, we’ve learned a lot. We now understand the importance of carefully weighing all options before choosing a solution, especially in areas where we don’t have much experience, and not diving in blindly.
